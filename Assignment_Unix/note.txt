The idea that everyone is treated equally in college, regardless of their financial background, is based on the ideal of meritocracy—the belief that academic ability, effort, and intelligence should be the only factors for success. While socioeconomic status often influences opportunities, college environments can temporarily create a space where, theoretically, a student’s performance matters more than their bank account.
